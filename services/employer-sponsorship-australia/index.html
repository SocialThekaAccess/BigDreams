What is Employer Sponsorship?

Employer sponsorship is when an Australian company sponsors a worker from another country for a job in Australia. This often happens when employers cannot find the right person locally and need someone with the right skills from overseas.

The visa type and job role can affect how long the applicant can work in Australia. Depending on the visa category, applicants may receive short-term or longer-term work opportunities.

The process may include:

  • Finding a suitable employer
  • Meeting eligibility requirements
  • Preparing documents
  • Skills and experience verification
  • Visa application process

Every applicant may have different requirements depending on their occupation and visa pathway.

Documents Required for Employer Sponsorship

Applicants usually need to prepare proper documents during the sponsorship and visa process.

Common documents may include:

  • Passport
  • Educational qualifications
  • Work experience documents
  • Resume or CV
  • English language test results
  • Employment-related documents
  • Identification documents

Requirements may vary depending on the visa category and occupation. Preparing documents correctly is important because incomplete information can create delays.

English Language Requirements

Some employer-sponsored visa pathways may require applicants to show English language ability through tests such as:

  • IELTS
  • PTE
  • TOEFL

Required scores may vary depending on the visa type and occupation category.
