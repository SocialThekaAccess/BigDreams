What Is a Skills Assessment?

A skills assessment is an official evaluation conducted by an authorised Australian assessing authority. It checks whether your qualifications, employment experience, and professional skills match Australian standards for your nominated occupation.

Assessing Authorities in Australia

Different occupations are assessed by different Australian assessing bodies. Choosing the correct authority is one of the most important parts of the process.

ACS

Australian Computer Society for ICT and technology professionals including software engineers, developers, programmers, and systems analysts.

Engineers Australia

For engineering occupations such as civil, electrical, mechanical, and electronics engineering.

VETASSESS

For a wide range of professional and trade occupations across multiple industries.

TRA

Trades Recognition Australia for skilled trades including chefs, electricians, plumbers, mechanics, and carpenters.

CPA Australia / CAANZ / IPA

For accounting and finance professionals.

ANMAC

For nursing and healthcare occupations.

Common Challenges Applicants Face

A lot of people who apply for migration feel really lost because each job has its own rules and its own way of doing things when it comes to Australia Migration Services.

Incorrect Occupation Selection

Selecting the wrong occupation or ANZSCO code may affect your eligibility and future visa application.

Incomplete Employment References

Employment letters must include detailed information such as job duties, working hours, salary, and employment duration.

Qualification Issues

Certain qualifications may not directly match Australian occupation standards.

Lack of Supporting Evidence

Some assessing authorities need evidence such as payslips, tax documents, project reports, and competency statements.

Technical Documentation Requirements

Occupations like engineering and IT may require career episodes or technical reports demonstrating professional skills.
